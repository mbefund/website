1914 Declaration of Purpose of Clause 8 Trust u/w Mary Baker Eddy

Mr Neal, Chairman of the Trustees under the will of Mrs Eddy, read the following statement: The Trustees under the will of Mary Baker Eddy, whose appointment and confirmation by the courts of New Hampshire took place a few months ago, have received substantially all of the property, the income of which Mrs Eddy in her will provided should be used for “more effectually promoting and extending the religion of Christian Science as taught by” her. In addition to this general provision named in her will, her trustees are especially fortunate in the possession of documents more specifically indicating the channels through which to accomplish the purposes intended. Final plans have not yet been perfected, but the principal lines of activity will be as follows:

  1. To contribute toward the expense of lectures given by Christian Science organizations outside the United States, Canada, and Great Britain.
  2. To contribute toward the ever-increasing distribution of authorized Christian Science literature throughout the world.
  3. To contribute toward the establishment and maintenance of circulating or lending libraries of authorized Christian Science literature in connection with Christian Science churches, societies, reading rooms and Sunday schools.
  4. To assist, in so far as is possible and practicable, in building Christian Science church edifices.

The Trustees are now engaged in obtaining information which will enable them to work out detailed plans for the furtherance of these objects. When these plans are more definitely formulated, further information will be given to the field.

CHRISTIAN SCIENCE SENTINEL

20 June 1914, p 825
