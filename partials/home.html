April 4, 2017

- Notice of Decision

Considerable progress has been made since the recent New Hampshire Probate Court hearing 20 March 2017. Judge David King of the 6th Circuit Court of New Hampshire (the senior Judge in Trust law in Probate Court) ruled on 4 April 2017 as follows:

  • He allowed the Trustees under the Will of Mary Baker Eddy to amend their financial accounts covering the past 13 years, although they were not independently audited; but will require trust funds to be independently audited from now on.
  • He confirmed that the Trustees violated the Court Stipulation Order for 2001 by not complying with the Court's requirement to audit trust funds.
  • He confirmed the violation by the Trustees of the Court order for their unapproved movement of funds, and required that any future movement of funds be approved by the Probate Court.
  • He recognised the embedded conflict of the fiduciary obligations that exist with the Trustees u/w of Mary Baker Eddy, who are also the Board of Directors of The Mother Church, and referred this conflict of interest matter to the Director of Charitable Trusts for further ruling.
  • He read, but formally declined to receive the Amicus Curiae brief (submitted by Second Church, Melbourne) on behalf of the Director of Charitable Trusts, as it was not necessary to the hearing; but encouraged Second Church to continue its work in support of the Director of Charitable Trusts.

Background

A most wonderful work is bearing fruit in this 151st year since the discovery of Christian Science by Mary Baker Eddy.

As background, Trust Deeds from 1903 – 1907, hand-written by court- appointed officers and signed by Mrs Eddy, were found in the New Hampshire Probate Court archives in 1997 through the work of a Christian Scientist from Lothian, Scotland. In 2000, the Documents were sent to Melbourne, Australia for further study and transcription. Upon deciphering the tightly hand-written documents, they were recognized as the key to our Pastor Emeritus’ administration of The Mother Church. These Deeds have not been available to Christian Science Church members since Mrs. Eddy’s passing, and reveal clear instruction to her followers.

Most importantly, the 1904 Trust Deed specifically states that "Nothing in this deed contained shall ever be construed as a waiver or as permitting a modification in any degree of any of the trusts" and further "no new tenet or by- law shall be adopted, nor any tenet or by-law amended or annulled, by the grantees".

Because of their importance, these Deeds of Trust, upon transcription, were immediately made available to the Christian Science Board of Directors in 2002 as required in Manual Article I, Section 9, and were later published on this web site. "It is the duty of any member of this Church, and especially of one who has been or who is the First Reader of a church, to inform the Board of Directors of the failure of the Committee on Publication or any other officer in this Church to perform his official duties." [Emphasis added]

It is now clear that over the decades, successive Boards of Directors/Trustees under the will, have been in default of their legal, religious, and fiduciary duties, because they - knowingly or unknowingly - violated our Leader’s will and Trust Deeds. The current Board of Directors have continued in these defaults.

Defaults include, but are not limited to:

  • The failure to recognize Mrs Eddy’s instructions in the December 21, 1903 deed, that they cannot self-appoint; that the Probate Court of New Hampshire must ratify and confirm the appointment of the Directors.
  • The failure to have the semi-annual accounts of trust funds audited, a mandatory requirement in New Hampshire. (Each retiring Trustee is individually responsible for the financial record.)
  • The failure to carry out and perform all the obligations imposed upon them, either as Directors or Trustees.
  • Denying the Pastor Emeritus and breaching her Church Manual.
  • Since 1949, the Board of Directors has stood as both a beneficiary (Clause 6) and Trustee (Clause 8) under Mrs Eddy’s will without the oversight of an independent administrator. This is a clear and serious conflict of interest, and has resulted, over time, in the corruption of the Pastor Emeritus’ Trusts. This has led to a cessation of funding to the field for the furtherance of Christian Science, as required under the Clause 8 Trust in her will. (See "1914 Purpose" under Trust Deeds and Documents tab.) This requirement was recognized by the Court and reinstated in 2018.

It is our prayer that this matter will revert to the Attorney General's statement of 1913, as can be read on this web site under "Fernald Decision", by clicking the heading Trust Deeds and Documents.

In that statement, the presiding Judge said:

"The testatrix [Mary Baker Eddy] intended the trust to be administered by persons professing the belief she desired to promote. Such persons it would be the duty of the court to appoint, should occasion arise, or at least none in hostility thereto should be permitted to undertake the execution of the trust."
"This language plainly carries with it the implication that it might become the duty of the court ‘to appoint persons not in hostility to the belief she desired to promote,’ other than these five directors."

All Mrs Eddy's Deeds of Trust are now speaking to the Court, who cannot deny her place, her gift, and her rights in her own Church. The demands and directions that Mrs Eddy places on the Trustees and Directors, in perpetuity, shine a clarifying light on her forever Leadership of her Church as its Pastor Emeritus.

We can now see the "full-orbed promise" of her Church - universal and triumphant - in action.
